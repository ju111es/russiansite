The genitive case is used to indicate possession, negation, origin, and amount- it answers questions like, “whose?”, “what is absent?”, and “of what?”. The genitive case could be considered the most complicated case in Russian, with myriad uses and no real pattern to the ending changes. (So it only gets easier from here!)

Add а to a hard consonant Replace а with ы Replace о with а Replace ь (feminine) with и
волк → волка женщина → женщины яблоко → яблока дверь → двери
Replace й with я Replace я with и Replace е with я Replace ь (masculine) with я
злодей → злодея змея → змеи море → моря словарь → словаря

The plural form also changes in the genitive case:

Add ов to a hard consonant Remove а Remove о Replace ь (feminine) with ей
волк → волков женщина → женщин яблоко → яблок дверь → дверей
Replace й with ев Replace [vowel] я with й Add й to е Replace ь (masculine) with ей
злодей → злодеев змея → змей море → морей словарь → словарей
Replace ж, ч, ш, щ with ей Replace [consonant] я with ь Replace ие with ий
муж → мужей кухня → кухонь* помишение → помишений

*For nouns ending in a, o, or я whose stems end in multiple consonants, an o or e is usually inserted between the consonants in the genitive plural. Кошка → кошек and окно → окон are other examples.

ЕГО

When directly preceded by e and directly followed by o, г sounds like в (a v sound instead of g). So, его is pronounced yevo. This is mostly present in the genitive case, as well as the accusative case which uses some genitive pronouns, and the masculine possessive pronoun его which is used in all cases.

Uses

There are a few prepositions which indicate that the corresponding noun will be in the genitive case:

без without у (indicates possession) о(т)from из from для for
кофе без молока у женщины есть кольца далеко от музея она из России это для моего пареня

The genitive case is also used with numbers and amounts:

1 nominative singular 2, 3, 4 genitive singular 5+ genitive plural 0 genitive plural
одна корова два, три, четыре коровы пять или больше коров нуль коров

When dealing with numbers bigger than 20, these rules apply to the last number:

сорок одна 41 корова сто девяносто три 193 коровы двенадцать 12 коров шестдесять 60 коров

The genitive case is also used with more general amounts like много a lot, немного a little, etc.
