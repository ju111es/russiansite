Russian has the same basic pronouns as English:

Я I, me Мы We, us
Ты You (singular/informal) Вы You (plural/formal)
Он He, him Они They, them
Она She, her
Оно It

The major difference between Russian and English here is that Russian has two words for you. Вы is used when you are addressing a group of people (you all), or when addressing a singular person you’d speak more formally to: an (adult) stranger; a teacher; a boss. Ты is used when speaking to someone you’re more familiar with, and only when addressing just one person.

These pronouns show ownership, and they change depending on the gender of the following noun:

My, mine Your, yours
(Ты)		 Our, ours You, yours
(Вы)		 One's own
masculine Мой Твой Наш Ваш Свой
feminine Моя Твоя Наша Ваша Своя
neuter Мое* Твое* Наше Ваше Свое*
plural Мои Твои Наши Ваши Свои

Его, ее*, and их are the masculine/neuter, feminine, and plural possessive pronouns, respectively. These aren't based on the gender of the following object (the item being possessed); rather, they are based on the gender of the noun they replace (the one possessing the object).

*You'll see any of the starred * pronouns spelled with either е or ё as the ending- these are interchangeable.

→ мой велосипед моя собака мое лицо мои дети
my bicycle my dog my face my children

These are demonstrative pronouns. They also change with the gender of the following noun:

This That All →
masculine Этот Тот Весь
feminine Эта Та Вся
neuter Это То Всё
plural Эти Те Все

Это can also mean this is. With this meaning, it always uses the neuter form:

это велосипед это собака
this is a bicycle this is a dog
это лицо это дети
this is a face these are children
этот велосипед
this bicycle
эта собака
this dog
это лицо
this face
эти дети
these children

Finally there are the pronouns что and кто, meaning what and who. These don’t change with gender, since they are naturally ambiguous- you don’t know the gender of what’s being referred to.

Like nouns, Russian pronouns change with grammatical case. These pronouns are in the nominative/default case.
