Verbs in Russian

Russian has many different infinitive verb forms, but they all fall into two conjugation groups, the first conjugation and the second conjugation. -ить verbs are always of the second conjugation; -ать, -еть, and -ять verbs are usually of the first conjugation, and all other verbs are of the first.

Verbs can be broken down as such:

stem + ending + ть

A few verbs have infinitives ending in -чь or -ти, but almost all of them end in -ть.

Conjugation Groups

When conjugating verbs, always drop the -ть and add the appropriate ending.


Verbs are conjugated according to their group:...

1st conjugation
Я stem + ending + ю Мы stem + ending + ем
Ты stem + ending + ешь Вы stem + ending + ете
Он(/а/о) stem + ending + ет Они stem + ending + ют
2nd conjugation
Я stem + ю Мы stem + им
Ты stem + ишь Вы stem + ите
Он(/а/о) stem + ит Они stem + ят

Endings

...While endings (in the first conjugation) change according to their infinitive form:

Some endings don't change: Some endings disappear: Some mutate into a vowel or consonant: And some switch vowels: In some endings, н is appended to the end:
ать → а ать → авать → а ыть → о деть → ден
ять → я оть → овать → у стать → стан
еть → е ереть → р
-нять verbs are their own thing: Finally, -чь and -ти verbs don't follow a precise pattern:
инять → им чь → г сти → т, б, or д сти → с
[vowel]нять → [vowel]йм чь → к сть → т, б, or д зти → з
[consonant]нять → [consonant]ним

Verbs in the second conjugation always drop their ending.

Notes

★ -ать verbs drop their ending after consonant mutation, and keep it otherwise.

★ Use у in place of ю when conjugating verbs with н appended to their ending, as well as -нуть, -нять, -ереть, and all -сть, -зть, and -ти verbs.

★ When conjugating -чь verbs, remember that their endings undergo consonant mutation on all but first person singular (я) and third person plural (они) conjugations (so, before e).

★ Only a few -деть verbs follow the above pattern. Consider the meaning when conjugating these verbs- prefixed versions of деть to put follow the pattern; others are regular еть verbs. For example, надеть to put on → наден

★ Unfortunately, there are always exceptions to the rules. See irregular verbs!!
