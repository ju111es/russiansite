Adjectives in Russian

An adjective's ending always matches the gender of the object they describe:

masculinefeminine neuterplural
-ый*-ая -ое-ые
вкусный супвкусная курица вкусное мясовкусные торты
tasty souptasty chicken tasty meattasty cakes

*Masculine nouns can also have other ending such as -ой and -ий; -ый is most common.

Like nouns and pronouns, adjectives change with grammatical case. These are in their nominative forms.


There are also soft adjectives, whose masculine forms end in -ний. These simply use soft vowels (я, е, и, ю) in place of hard ones (а, о, ы, у). This is true in every grammatical case.

masculinefeminine neuterplural
-ий-яя -ее-ие
средний ребёноксредняя школа среднее имя*средние сиденья
middle childmiddle school middle namemiddle seats

*Имя is a neuter noun with an irregular (seemingly feminine) ending.


Adjectives can be shortened for simple, subject-adjective sentences (for example: that building is old; this woman is kind; my dog is cute).

masculinefeminine neuterplural
--а -о-ы
красив храмкрасива птица красиво солнцекрасивы люди
beautiful templebeautiful bird beautiful sunbeautiful people

Comparative adjectives

There are a few way to compare nouns using adjectives.

Using более more / менее less + чем than

Этот суп менее вкусный, чем тот.
This soup is less tasty than that one.

Ее картина более красивая, чем его картина.
Her painting is more beautiful than his.

As you can see, the adjective still matches the gender of the noun.
